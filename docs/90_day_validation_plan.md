# 90-day validation plan

| Phase | Question | Design | Primary metric | Guardrails | Decision rule |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Weeks 1–2 | Do consumers understand and value the concepts? | 5–10 interviews + concept survey | Purchase consideration / relevance | Confusion, perceived risk | Keep only concepts with clear comprehension and a meaningful relevance gap |
| Weeks 3–6 | Does a discovery/travel route reduce purchase risk? | Randomized landing/PDP A/B test | Trial-kit purchase conversion; trial-to-full-size conversion | Refund, cancel, customer-service contact rate | Scale only if conversion improves and guardrails do not materially worsen |
| Weeks 7–10 | Does occasion × scent × performance content move qualified action? | Randomized content/creative cells | Product-page click-through and qualified engagement | Hide/mute rate, bounce rate | Require consistent lift across at least two creative variants |
| Weeks 11–12 | Which segment and price band should scale? | Pre-specified subgroup readout | Incremental conversion by segment/price band | Small-sample uncertainty, multiple testing | Expand only pre-specified, adequately powered segments |

## Metric formulas

- Trial purchase conversion = trial purchasers / eligible visitors.
- Trial-to-full-size conversion = full-size purchasers within 30/60/90 days /
  trial purchasers eligible for follow-up.
- Qualified engagement rate = saves + meaningful comments + product clicks /
  content impressions. Platform definitions must be fixed before the test.
- Refund/cancel guardrail = refunded or cancelled orders / placed orders.

The plan should pre-register eligibility, sample-size assumptions, minimum
detectable effect, analysis window and stopping rule. Observational review
patterns prioritize tests; they do not establish the causal effect of an
intervention.
